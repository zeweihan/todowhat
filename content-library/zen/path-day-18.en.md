## Original Passage

*Fresh translation from Classical Chinese.*

Yunmen took up his staff and, showing it to the assembly, said:

"The staff has transformed into a dragon and swallowed the three realms whole. Mountains, rivers, the great earth — where have they come from?"

---

Xuedou's verse:

> Short staff, long staff — take it away and what?  
> Blue sky, white sun — nothing hidden.  
> When you find it, carry it.  
> When it finds you — put it down.

## Modern Reading

A staff is a staff. Yunmen picks it up and names it a dragon. The dragon swallows everything — mountains, rivers, the whole ground under your feet. And then his question: where did all of it come from? Not where did it go — it was swallowed. Where did it come from? The answer cannot be the staff. The answer cannot be the dragon. The assembly is silent because any answer reaches back for ground that has already been swallowed. This is presence at its most radical: nothing to stand on except this.

## Commentary

The staff lies across Yunmen's palm. Before he picks it up, it is just wood. When he holds it and speaks, it swallows the universe.

What changed?

Direct mind — 直心 — is Huineng's term for the quality of attention that does not take detours. It does not stop at the name of a thing before touching the thing. It does not wait for permission from a concept before acting. Yunmen's staff is held with direct mind. That is why it can be, in the same gesture, wood and dragon and nothing at all.

The line Xuedou gives is the one to carry today: "When you find it, carry it. When it finds you — put it down." The staff is not a possession. Presence is not a possession. You cannot decide to be present and stay decided. You can only pick up the staff when your hand is right there.

Most of what we call distraction is the staff lying on the floor while we think about it.

This moment — this specific one, not the idea of being present — requires nothing but your actual attention. Not your intention to pay attention. Your attention itself.

Pick up the staff.

## Socratic Prompts

1. Look at an ordinary object near you right now — a cup, a phone, a hand. Without naming what it is, what do you actually see? How long can you hold that direct contact before the name rushes back?
2. Yunmen says the staff swallowed the mountains and the great earth. Right now, in this room, what would it mean for your attention to be so complete that it swallowed the room?
3. Xuedou says: "When it finds you — put it down." When were you last found by presence rather than chasing it? What were you doing?
4. You have been practicing for 18 days. Is there a version of this practice you have built in your mind — an ideal of how it should feel — that is now getting in the way of how it actually feels today?
5. The staff is just wood until Yunmen holds it. What in your life right now — a task, a conversation, a problem — is lying inert because you have not yet brought your actual attention to it?
6. "Mountains, rivers, the great earth — where have they come from?" Answer this question with your body, not your mind. Notice where the question lands in you, physically, before you try to think about it.
7. At this exact moment, how much effort are you making to be present? And is the effort helping or is it itself the obstacle to what you are looking for?
