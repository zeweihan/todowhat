## Original Passage

> Good friends, in this teaching of mine, from ancient times to the present, all have set up non-thought (無念) as the main doctrine, non-form (無相) as the substance, and non-abiding (無住) as the basis.
>
> Non-form means: to be free from form even when associated with form. Non-thought means: to have no thought even when involved in thought. Non-abiding is the original nature of man.
>
> Good friends, not to allow the mind to be contaminated while in contact with all things — this is called non-thought. In our own thought we should always be free from the influence of things; but do not cease from thinking, for the moment you do so, you become tied to a single spot.

## Modern Reading

Sitting still is not the teaching. Clearing the mind is not the teaching. What Huineng points at is stranger and more available than either: a way of being present that is not held in place by any object — not even the object of silence, not even the idea of peace. The thought arises. The mind does not become the thought.

## Commentary

This is where most people get stuck.

You sit. Thoughts arise. You try to push them away. They return. You label them distraction. You judge yourself for having them. The effort to stop thinking becomes its own persistent thought — louder than the ones it was trying to quiet.

Huineng is pointing somewhere beyond this loop.

Non-thought — 無念 — is not a state you manufacture by force. It is closer to what is already present when you stop adding anything. A cloud moves through open sky. The sky does not become cloudy. The thought arises, passes. The mind does not become the thought, unless it reaches for it.

This is subtle. Easy to mistake for passivity, or for an excuse to stop trying. It is neither.

What Huineng describes is a quality of presence that is not directed at anything. Not at peace, not at clarity, not even at the absence of thought. Something that holds whatever arises — without capture, without comment.

When you sit today, the goal is not to produce stillness. The goal is to notice what is already still underneath the movement.

That is already here.
