## Original Passage

> Good friends, this teaching of mine takes no-thought (無念) as its tenet, non-form (無相) as its substance, and non-attachment (無住) as its foundation.
>
> What is non-form? Non-form means to be among forms and yet be free of form.
>
> What is no-thought? No-thought means to think without stopping at the thought.
>
> What is non-attachment? Non-attachment means: in the midst of each thing, not to dwell there.
>
> The past moment has already gone. The future moment has not yet come. Each present moment of mind — do not let it rest on the last, or wait for the next. This is the teaching.

## Modern Reading

Three technical terms that look like instructions to empty yourself. They are not. Huineng is not asking you to stop thinking, stop perceiving, stop engaging. He is pointing at something much more specific: the difference between moving through experience and accumulating it. Each wave of mind passes. The one that passed is gone. The one coming has not arrived. The one here — stop. What is it?

## Commentary

You are at the midpoint of this path. Fifteen days in.

Huineng's three terms — no-thought, non-form, non-attachment — are easy to memorize and almost impossible to live. Most practitioners turn them into ideals. "I should think less. I should be less attached." That is Shenxiu's mistake again — treating the mirror as a surface to polish.

The teaching is narrower than that. Each moment of mind arises and passes. The practice is not to stop the arising. It is to stop the stockpiling — the way each new moment gets added to a running account of who you are and how it is going.

"Non-attachment as the foundation" does not mean detachment. It means: each wave of experience is allowed to be complete. The previous moment does not contaminate this one.

Right now — not as a principle, but as a fact — the practice session you had yesterday is not in the room. Your concern about tomorrow is only a thought arising in this one. What remains when you stop adding to the account?

Sit with that. Not as a question. As an investigation.

## Socratic Prompts

1. Right now, as you sit here — not what you feel in general, but this specific moment — what is the texture of your attention? Is it sharp, dull, scattered, still? Describe it as precisely as you can without evaluating it.
2. Notice your breath. Not breathing in general — this inhale, right now. How far into your body does it reach? Do you actually know, or are you imagining?
3. Huineng says: "do not let the present moment rest on the last, or wait for the next." In the last ten minutes, how many times did your mind leave this room? What pulled it?
4. There is a part of you that has been keeping score on this practice — tracking whether you are "improving." What does that scorekeeper feel like in your body, right now?
5. Think of the last time you were genuinely, completely in a moment — not reflecting on being in it, just in it. What made that possible? Was it effort, or its absence?
6. This moment will be gone in a second. The next one will arrive. You have no control over either. What do you actually have control over, right now, in this room?
7. When you finish this session, you will close the app and re-enter your day. What is the last thing you will bring out with you — not an idea, a felt quality? Where in your body is it right now?
