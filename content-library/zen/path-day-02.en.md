## Original Passage

*Fresh translation from Classical Chinese.*

The head monk Shenxiu wrote on the wall:

*The body is the Bodhi tree.*
*The mind is like a bright mirror on its stand.*
*Polish it diligently at all times*
*and let no dust alight.*

Later, Huineng had someone read the verse to him, and he asked to add his own. He could not write, so he had someone transcribe:

*Bodhi has no tree.*
*The bright mirror has no stand.*
*Originally, there is not a single thing —*
*where could dust alight?*

The Fifth Patriarch saw the second verse and wiped it away with his sandal. "This person has not yet seen nature," he said — loud enough for all to hear. But at midnight, he summoned Huineng alone, and passed the robe and bowl to him.

## Modern Reading

Two monks, two views of mind. Shenxiu sees practice as maintenance: the mirror of awareness must be kept clean. Huineng sees that the mirror never existed as a thing to be polished — not because the mind is nonexistent, but because its nature is already empty of any fixed substance. The Fifth Patriarch pretends to dismiss Huineng's verse while secretly confirming it as the deeper seeing.

## Commentary

It is worth sitting with Shenxiu's verse for a moment before dismissing it. There is nothing wrong with it. It describes real experience: thoughts accumulate, the mind grows clouded, effort is needed. Most practitioners spend years — productive, useful years — working exactly this way. Shenxiu himself was a highly accomplished monk, later revered as the founder of a major school.

And yet. The Fifth Patriarch chose the verse written by the illiterate kitchen worker, not the polished verse of the senior scholar. The reason is precise: Shenxiu is describing the correct management of something that is already there. He hasn't questioned what the mirror is. Huineng points earlier — to before the mirror, to before the practitioner who polishes. From that vantage, there is no dust to remove because there is no surface for dust to land on.

This is not a negation of practice. It is a reorientation of its ground. You polish a mirror when you believe the mirror is real. What happens when you look for the mirror itself?

## Socratic Prompts

1. When you examine your own mind right now — the thing doing the observing — can you find it as an object? Does awareness have a location, a texture, an edge?
2. *Koan: "Originally, there is not a single thing." — What is the thing that read this sentence?*
