## Original Passage

*Fresh translation from Classical Chinese.*

Damei asked the great master Mazu: "What is Buddha?"

Mazu said: "This mind is Buddha."

---

*Wumen's Commentary:*
If you can grasp this directly, you are wearing Buddha's clothes, eating Buddha's food, speaking Buddha's words, performing Buddha's conduct. You are Buddha. But Wanglaoshi has fooled not a few people with this "This mind is Buddha." You must know: to avoid the trap, close your teeth.

---

*Wumen's Verse:*
Under a clear sky, in bright sunlight,
do not go searching around.
If you ask what Buddha is —
this voice holds the whole teaching.

## Modern Reading

The week closes with the most direct formulation in the Zen canon: your mind is Buddha. Not a representation of Buddha. Not a capacity for Buddhahood. This mind — the one reading these words, the one that wandered this week, the one that doubts — is it. Wumen adds a warning immediately: do not be fooled. The statement is a gate, not a conclusion.

## Commentary

Seven days into this path, a week of looking — and the teaching lands here: this mind is Buddha. It seems like an arrival. But Wumen's commentary does something interesting. He celebrates and then immediately warns against it. "Wanglaoshi has fooled not a few people." Who is Wanglaoshi? It is a semi-affectionate title meaning "the old master" — Mazu himself. The teaching that opened something in Damei is, for others, a comfortable place to stop.

"This mind is Buddha" can be heard as permission to do nothing. You are already it; why practice? This interpretation is what Wumen calls the trap. The statement is not a conclusion. It is the beginning of a sharper looking. Which mind? Not the mind that plans, not the mind that narrates your experience, not the mind that decided Zen was interesting. Those are activities of mind. What Mazu points at is the nature of mind before it takes any particular shape.

This week has moved through Buddha-nature, the mirror, vast emptiness, a dirty bowl, a gate with no gate, and a syllable with no meaning. All of it circling the same unreachable object: what is actually here, before you name it? Damei asked a question and received an answer so complete it left nothing to add. Can you hear it the same way?
