## Original Passage

> "Good friends, the Mahaprajnaparamita is a Sanskrit term of the Western region. In our language, it means great wisdom that reaches the other shore.
>
> This teaching must be practiced with the heart; it is not a matter of reciting. If you only recite without practice, you will be like a phantom, like dew, like lightning. The recitation and the practice must be one.
>
> Good friends, what is prajna? In our own original nature, there is no ignorance and no wisdom; but sometimes confused, sometimes enlightened. The confused state is the state of the ordinary person. The enlightened state is the state of the sage. But there is no difference in nature. Only in application."

## Modern Reading

The teaching is not in the words. Huineng says this plainly: recite without practicing and you remain where you are. The words point at something that words cannot carry. Prajna — wisdom — is not a concept to be accumulated. It is the nature that is already present, before confusion and before its clearing.

## Commentary

There is a moment before you speak that contains everything you are about to say.

That moment does not belong to language. It is not yet organized into words, grammar, sequence. And yet it is full — full of whatever you are about to know, about to offer, about to discover you understand.

Huineng spent his life pointing at that moment.

Everything in this practice reaches you through language: passages, commentary, questions, your own reflection written back to you on a screen. Words pointing at what words cannot hold. The risk is that you begin to confuse the pointing for the destination. That you accumulate ideas about the nature of mind rather than meeting the nature of mind.

The wordless teaching is not silence as absence. It is silence as fullness — the condition in which the real instruction can land.

Prajna cannot be given. It cannot be received. It is recognized. You hear the teaching and something already in you says: yes, this is how it is. That recognition is the teaching. Not the words that triggered it.

In your sitting today, let the commentary go. Let the session go. What remains, underneath the words, is what these words were always reaching for.

Sit there, for a few minutes. Without reaching.
