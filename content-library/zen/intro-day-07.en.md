## Original Passage

From Chapter 10, Huineng's final teaching to his disciples before his passing:

> "Good friends, each of you must attend carefully to what I say, and pass this teaching on for generations to come.
>
> Know that your self-nature is fundamentally pure and clear. It is neither born nor destroyed. It neither increases nor decreases. It does not come and go. This nature is not confused by delusion and it is not clarified by enlightenment. It is not in the ordinary person, and it is not in the sage. It has no location in south or north, east or west. It is neither coming nor going. Your original nature is right here — do not seek it elsewhere.
>
> Good friends, those who know their own minds and see their own original nature — they have found it. There is nowhere else to look."

Traditional question attributed to Huineng's lineage, preserved in this chapter's commentary tradition:

> "What was your face before your parents were born?"

## Modern Reading

Seven days. This is the last one of the introduction, and Huineng uses his last words to say the same thing he said at the beginning — in a different key. Original nature is not a destination. It was never something to be reached. The face you had before your parents were born is not the face of a ghost from the past. It is what is looking out right now, beneath everything that has accumulated on top of it.

## Commentary

Before your parents met, there was no one to name you. Before you were born, you had no habits built from a lifetime of small decisions, no story accumulated across decades. No preferences shaped by approval and loss.

This is not a thought experiment in non-existence. It is a direction.

What Huineng called "original nature" is not something you achieve. It is not what you become after enough practice, enough years, enough insight. It is the base layer that all your accumulations have been resting on — without touching it, without changing it.

The question is not asking you to imagine your non-existence. It is asking you to find what was present before the self-story began. Before "I am the kind of person who." Before "I am trying to become." Before the mirror had anything to reflect.

You have spent seven days now sitting inside this tradition. Each session a single stroke. Together, an introduction — not to a subject, but to a practice you will be inside for the rest of your life if you choose it.

The original face is not at the end of a longer path. It is what has been looking out through your eyes this whole time.

What do you see?
