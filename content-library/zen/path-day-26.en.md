## Original Passage

A disciple asked Huineng: "After you are gone, to whom shall we look for instruction?"

Huineng replied:

> Good friends, since you have asked, I will tell you. After my departure, if you wish to find a teacher, seek within your own self-nature. Do not seek outside. Outside there is no Buddha, no dharma. The dharma is not in the east, not in the west, not in the north, not in the south. It does not wait for you in a monastery or on a mountain.

> Your original nature is pure and complete. It lacks nothing. When you are hungry, eat. When you are tired, sleep. When there is water to carry, carry it. Do not pursue the marvelous at the expense of the ordinary.

> Those who understand this need not be anxious about whether they practice correctly. Their walking is practice. Their sitting is practice. Their carrying and their setting down — all of it is practice.

## Modern Reading

The Master is leaving. His disciples want to know where to go next. He tells them: nowhere. The teaching is in what your hands are doing right now.

## Commentary

Huineng's answer to his disciples' anxiety is almost startlingly simple. You are looking for a successor to transmit the teaching. The teaching is in the carrying.

This does not mean that anything you do counts as practice. It means that when you are carrying water — actually carrying it, weight in the hands, attention on the path, the sloshing sound inside the bucket — there is nothing that practice needs to add.

The difficulty is that most of the time we are not carrying water. We are thinking about what comes after the water is delivered. We are rehearsing a conversation. We are adding a commentary to our actions as we perform them, evaluating whether we are doing it right, wondering whether this counts.

The disciple's question — "to whom shall we look?" — is itself the problem. To look outward for instruction is to assume the instruction is somewhere you haven't been yet. Huineng's entire life was a refusal of that assumption.

There is water to carry today. Not as a metaphor for something else. As water, path, bucket, hands, weight.
