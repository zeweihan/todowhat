## Original Passage

A monk asked Dongshan: "What is Buddha?"

Dongshan said: "Three pounds of hemp."

---

Wumen's commentary:

> Old Dongshan attained a bit of clam Zen — just opens the shell and shows the liver and intestines. But though that is so, tell me: where do you see Dongshan?

Wumen's verse:

> "Three pounds of hemp" comes suddenly forth.  
> Close is the word, closer still the meaning.  
> He who argues about right and wrong  
> Is himself a man of right and wrong.

## Modern Reading

A monk asks the most important question in Buddhism. The teacher answers with what he is weighing at that moment — three pounds of hemp thread, the material in his hands. This is not a riddle. It is the teaching.

## Commentary

You could spend a week interpreting this case. Commentators have.

The answer must mean emptiness — because Buddha-nature is beyond form and three pounds of hemp is plainly material. Or the answer must mean the opposite — that Buddha-nature pervades even mundane objects; the hemp is sacred because everything is. Or perhaps Dongshan was simply busy and gave a distracted answer, and the distraction itself is the teaching.

All of these readings are attempts to move away from the bare fact: a monk asked a sincere question, and a man said three pounds of hemp.

What is striking is how ordinary the answer is. Not "everywhere" or "nothing" or "ask your own heart." Just the weight of what is in his hands right now. In this moment, working, weighing, occupied with the texture of things — here is Buddha.

Week 4 is asking you to stay in the hemp. Not the symbol of the hemp, not the spiritual significance of weighing material, not what hemp teaches us about non-attachment. The hemp itself, in the hands, with its particular smell and weight.

Most of us live just slightly above our experience — narrating, evaluating, planning the next move. Dongshan lives inside it. That is the difference Wumen is pointing at.

## Socratic Prompts

1. What are the three pounds of hemp in your hands today — the specific, material, unglamorous task that is actually in front of you right now? Is there a way to meet it without putting anything else on top of it?
